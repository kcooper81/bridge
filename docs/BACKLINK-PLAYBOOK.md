# TeamPrompt Backlink Playbook

> **State as of 2026-05-15:** Site is ~6 months old, ~90 days indexed. GSC trend: position improved page 3 → page 2 over the last 28 days, but only ~530 monthly impressions. The technical SEO foundation is solid; the next leverage point is **earned authority signals** (backlinks, mentions, directory presence). This doc is the execution playbook.

---

## How to use this doc

The directories and outreach targets below are sorted by **ROI** = (estimated traffic + DR juice) ÷ (effort to submit). Start at the top and work down. Each entry has:

- **Where**: exact URL and account requirement
- **What to submit**: the value to paste
- **Effort**: minutes of work
- **DR / DA**: domain authority — higher is better, ranges are estimates from public crawls
- **Why this one**: what it specifically gives TeamPrompt (referral traffic, SERP juice, citation, etc.)

Skip anything that sounds spammy. Every link should be defensible if a Google reviewer looked at it.

## Tier 5 — Programmatic content / longtail (the slow-burn play)

### 24. Comparison content on partner sites
Reach out to bloggers who have written "X tool comparison" posts within the last 6 months — offer them updated info, screenshots, a unique data point. They'll often update the article with a new mention.

**Tools to find these**:
- `site:medium.com "ChatGPT for teams" "alternatives"` (Google query)
- `site:reddit.com "AI prompt manager"` (find threads where you can helpfully comment)
- Ahrefs / Semrush keyword research (need a paid account)

### 25. Wikipedia mention (if you can earn it)
- **Where**: Pages on "Data loss prevention", "Prompt engineering", "Browser extension"
- **Effort**: Significant — Wikipedia editors are strict; you cannot self-promote. Best path: **publish original research** (e.g. "the 2026 Shadow AI Report — survey of 500 CISOs") that gets cited in news articles, then a third party may add the citation to Wikipedia.
- **Why**: Wikipedia DR 100, almost any link from there is gold.

## Ongoing tactics

### Original research = link magnet
Run a quarterly survey: "State of AI in the Workplace" — 500-1000 respondents, real data, charts. Publish as a free PDF gated only by an email. Pitch the findings to journalists with the Tier 4 outlets above. Each citation in a news article is a dofollow backlink + brand mention.

This is the single highest-leverage long-term tactic. Companies like LayerX, Cyera, and Dig (now CrowdStrike) built their entire authority on quarterly research reports.

### Customer logo wall + case studies
Once you have 3+ paying customers willing to be named, publish case studies. Each customer might cross-link from their "tools we use" or "vendors" page (B2B sites often have these).

### Broken link recovery
Every few months, run a check on your top competitors' brand-name searches. When a blog post mentions them but doesn't mention you, email the author with "I noticed you covered X tools but missed Y — here's why TeamPrompt might fit." Conversion rate is low (~5%) but every win is a single high-quality backlink.
